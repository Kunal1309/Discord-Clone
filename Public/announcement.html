IMPORTANT
Hello folks!
The community has grown so much in the last year. The Odin Project's website reaches nearly double the number of people it did a year prior, and our Discord Server has completely exploded and more than doubled in the same period of time. We are incredibly happy to be able to reach millions a year and help them achieve their dreams. We thank you for being a part of this community! With this period of incredible growth brings a new set of challenges when it comes to moderating the community, and making this a safe and productive environment for learners to garner the skills needed to thrive and excel in their first software engineering role. As a team, we have considered a revamp of the moderation techniques and approaches for quite some time now, researching many different tools, options, and moderation styles, and as we reach a period of critical mass, now is the time to roll out some changes. We have developed a moderation matrix, based on the moderation systems of other servers of the same size or larger. This system tracks infractions by severity and frequency, and helps users understand the limitations of the rules and what rules cause what kind of action. It also helps moderators become more objective when enforcing the rules and doling out appropriate moderation action, and attempts to limit subjectivity and personal opinion. We chose this approach because we wanted to uniformly protect our community and remove as much subjectivity in enforcing the rules as we can (though we are still human and understand certain situations should be handled with context in mind). We also chose this approach as it has been seen to be very effective in communities larger than our own, and our highest priority here is to develop strong, curious, effective problem solvers, and our ability to do so is strongly impacted by the culture of our server, which has proven to be an effective tool for learners going through our curriculum. Our next highest priority is to protect the users here, both on the team and learners going through TOP. It is very important to us to have a safe, welcoming, and peaceful environment for all. Please review the matrix, and let us know if you have any feedback in the Dyno form below. All feedback should go through the Dyno form at this time. Do note that the entire moderation team will be able to see your feedback, as well as the Maintainer and Core team, so please be mindful that there are humans on the other side of the screen. The matrix will become effective on Sunday, March 20. It is not retroactive, everyone will start on a clean slate! Matrix: https://github.com/TheOdinProject/top-meta/blob/main/MODERATION/Moderation-Matrix.md Dyno form: https://dyno.gg/form/347620e

Hey @everyone ! We have noticed an uptick in “Play my game scams.”. If you are unfamiliar with this kind of scam/dangerous link you can read more about it on this Reddit post https://www.reddit.com/r/discordapp/comments/s1f1vs/the_recent_try_my_game_discord_scam_explained/. Please be aware that if you receive a link or someone posts a link in the chat regardless of them being a mod, maintainer, core member or even a regular member of the discord do NOT click the link without checking the link for misspellings or other signs of it being illegitimate. Often these links look like legitimate links to either Discord, GitHub, or another legitimate site. It’s important to keep your account safe and a good way to do that is to view any link or download as suspicious and double-check it comes from a legitimate source. If you want more tips to keep yourself in regards to links you can check this out as well https://secure360.org/2017/12/spot-suspicious-links/. As well if you see any obvious “Play my game scams” feel free to send a DM to Modmail or warn other individuals not to click the link. Stay safe everyone!
